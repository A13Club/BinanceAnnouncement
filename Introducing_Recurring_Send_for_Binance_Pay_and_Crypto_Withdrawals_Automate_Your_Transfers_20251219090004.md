# Introducing Recurring Send for Binance Pay and Crypto Withdrawals: Automate Your Transfers

This is a general announcement. Products and services referred to here may not be available in your region. Terms and conditions apply. 

Fellow Binancians,

Binance is excited to introduce two powerful Recurring Send features for Binance Pay and crypto withdrawals. These new functionalities are designed to automate your regular crypto transfers and withdrawals, streamlining your financial routines and providing greater convenience and control.

Recurring Send on Binance Pay

The Recurring Send feature within Binance Pay empowers users to automate regular payments to trusted Binance recipients. Streamline routines like subscriptions or allowances by scheduling recurring fund transfers—set it once and funds will be sent on time, every time.

Key Highlights:

Automated Scheduled Payments: Set up and schedule recurring fund transfers to designated recipients at customized intervals (e.g., daily, weekly, monthly), eliminating the need for manual payments.Flexible Recipient Selection: Send recurring payments to other Binance users by entering their Binance UID, email, or phone number. You can also select from recent payees, or choose contacts directly from your phonebook.Timely Notifications and Reminders: Receive reminders one day before a scheduled payment and get instant notification if a transaction fails due to an insufficient balance, ensuring you stay informed.Easy Management and Control: Conveniently view, modify, or cancel your active Recurring Send plans at any time.

How to Set Up Recurring Send:

Users may access this function via the Binance App.

Step 1: Log in to your Binance account via the App. Navigate to the [Pay] homepage section and choose [Recurring Send].

Step 2: Alternatively, go to [Send to Binance users] on the Binance Pay homepage, then choose [Recurring Send] at the top of the Send page.Step 3: Select your recipient via their email, phone number, or Binance UID (BUID).

*Please note this feature is only available for transfers to other Binance accounts.

Step 4: Enter the amount and select the cryptocurrency you wish to send.Step 5: Customize your plan by setting the first send date, frequency (e.g., daily, weekly, monthly) and the last send date.Step 6: Review the details of your Recurring Send plan and tap [Confirm] to schedule your automated payments.

Recurring Send for Crypto Withdrawals

Automate and schedule your crypto withdrawals to other Binance users or external on-chain addresses. This feature simplifies asset management by enabling flexible, recurring transfers at daily, weekly, or monthly intervals, ensuring timely and secure transactions with minimal manual effort.

Key Highlights:

Flexible Scheduling Options: Set up automated crypto withdrawals on a daily, weekly, or monthly basis for consistent and convenient asset transfers.Support for Binance and On-Chain Addresses: Enables transfers to other Binance users or designated external blockchain addresses.Withdrawal Whitelist Compliance: Ensures added security by restricting scheduled withdrawals to whitelisted addresses when the feature is enabled.Multiple Schedules Management: Supports setting up multiple concurrent recurring withdrawal schedules for different assets or recipients.Balance Optimization: Offers the option to utilize alternative assets when the primary token balance is insufficient, reducing transfer failures.

How to Set Up Recurring Send for Crypto Withdrawals:

Users may access this function via both the Binance website and App.

Step 1: Log in to your Binance account via the App or website. Navigate to the [Services] homepage section, then choose [Recurring Send].

Step 2: Alternatively, go to [Crypto Withdrawal page], then open the dropdown list under your selected token.Step 3: Choose a preferred frequency from daily, weekly, monthly, or annually.Step 4: Paste an external on-chain withdrawal address or choose a saved address from your address book.Step 5: Enter the amount and select [Recurring Withdraw] to schedule your automated withdrawals.
